10.Sword Art Online

"Sword Art Online" (SAO) is a Japanese light novel series turned multimedia franchise that has captivated audiences worldwide. Set in a virtual reality MMORPG (Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game), the series follows protagonist Kirito as he navigates through various virtual worlds, battling enemies and forging bonds with fellow players. With its engaging storyline, rich character development, and stunning animation, SAO has garnered a massive following since its inception. The anime adaptation, in particular, has amassed 30 millions of views globally, cementing its status as one of the most popular anime series of the modern era. Its success has spawned numerous spin-offs, movies, and video games, further solidifying its place in pop culture history.

9.Haikyu

Haikyu!! (ハイキュー!!, Haikyū!!, from the kanji 排球 "volleyball") is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Haruichi Furudate. It was serialized in Shueisha's shōnen manga magazine Weekly Shōnen Jump from February 2012 to July 2020, with its chapters collected in 45 tankōbon volumes. The story follows Shoyo Hinata, a boy determined to become a great volleyball player despite his small stature.An anime television series produced by Production I.G aired from April 6 to September 21, 2014 on MBS, other JNN stations, and with English subtitles on Crunchyroll. From episodes 1-13, the opening theme song is "Imagination" by Spyair, while the ending theme song is "Tenchi Gaeshi" by Nico Touches the Walls. For episodes 14 through 25, the opening is "Ah Yeah" by Sukima Switch, and the ending is "LEO" by Tacica.
